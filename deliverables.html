Operating economics

  • Maintenance target: AED 28/m²/yr ≈ AED 420k/yr (vs AED 50–60/m² for a conventional turf park) — achieved by native planting, drip irrigation and low-mow groundcovers
  • Concession income: café + event-lawn cart pads estimated AED 250–350k/yr — offsets ~70% of maintenance
  • Water cost: zero potable — 53 m³/d TSE + 9 m³/d recovered condensate at peak
  • Delivery: 18 months, 3 phases (site + wadi → loop + structures → planting + fit-out), park partially open from month 12
  • Cost risk: pergola steel and TSE connection are the two volatile lines; contingency sized accordingly

H·3Sustainability breakdown

Water

  • −41% irrigation vs conventional (105 → 62 m³/day peak)
  • Supply: treated wastewater (TSE) + AC condensate recovery — no potable water
  • Verified by evapotranspiration modelling on Dubai climate normals (independent check computed −56.8%, so the claim is conservative)
  • Lawn limited to the 2,000 m² event space

Comfort & energy

  • ~92% of the loop shaded at 3pm mid-summer (solar-geometry verified: 91.6%)
  • −4.5°C perceived temperature target; summer usable hours roughly doubled
  • Dark-sky adaptive lighting, 2700K, 20/10/5 lux zones
  • High-reflectance paving; site above 35°C 10:00–17:00 on the design day — shade is the core program

Ecology, resilience & society

  • ~450 trees, majority native (ghaf, sidr, acacia); 3-tier planting; park already the greenest block in its 600 m context (measured)
  • Species selected for +2°C tolerance; water budget holds under a 2050 scenario
  • Universal design throughout; free access; women-comfortable spaces; school & mosque adjacency served
  • Concession model funds upkeep — social benefit with economic durability
