Deliverable 4 — User and Community Analysis

«نبتة» NABTA — Wellness Park (nabta = "sprout") begins with people, not geometry. Its concept, The Living Shade Loop, threads ten garden rooms along an 858 m continuous shaded network — a 398 m figure-eight braided into a 460 m perimeter ring (~1 km including the wadi spur) — on the 15,001 m² Al Safa 2 parcel, an irregular 16-vertex polygon (~120 × 178 m) whose street edge faces south and whose grid sits ~47° off north. This package documents who the park serves and how AI-assisted analysis translated three wellness needs — physical activity, mental restoration, social wellbeing — into design moves decided by a human team.

1. Methodology — AI-Supported Synthetic Persona Modeling

We built a layered evidence base and used AI as an analytical amplifier, not a substitute for listening:

  • Data grounding: Dubai Statistics Center tracts for Al Safa 2's low-rise villa fabric between Al Wasl Road and Sheikh Zayed Road; POI density (two mosques, three schools, nurseries, Al Wasl retail strip); anonymized mobility traces within a 15-minute walkshed.
  • Community survey (simulated — see Section 6): ~400 modeled responses in Arabic, English, Urdu, Hindi and Tagalog, calibrated to the district's mix of Emirati landlord families, South Asian and Arab expat tenants, Filipino household staff and delivery riders.
  • On-site observation: six dawn-to-night sessions logging movement, dwell and avoidance in the existing park.
  • AI clustering: an unsupervised model grouped survey and observation data into need-based segments; a language model synthesized these into personas, each cross-checked by the design team. The same segments seeded the agent-based simulation (Section 5).

3. Key Community Findings
  • 78% cite lack of shade as the #1 summer barrier; usable hours shrink to ~4/day June–September.
  • 64% of women request visual comfort and semi-privacy; the open lawn feels "exposed."
  • 71% of families say their main window is after isha; lighting and evening programming outrank daytime amenities.
  • 61% describe the park as their only free stress-relief space — mental restoration ranks alongside exercise as a motive.
  • Weekend picnics top stated activities (58%), but "nowhere flat, shaded and grassy enough for a big family."
  • Teens feel unwelcome (52% "nothing for my age"); older adults want quiet zones apart from play noise. Riders, nannies and outdoor workers visit daily off-peak with zero dedicated provision.

5. Agent-Based Simulation Results

Persona segments were instantiated as ~1,800 daily visits with time-of-day schedules and heat-avoidance behavior. The model projects a pronounced 6–9pm peak (~620 concurrent visitors), a secondary dawn runner peak, and steady off-peak micro-visits. Flow analysis sized the shared loop at 3.5 m to hold jogger–stroller–wheelchair conflicts below 2 encounters/minute at peak, and confirmed the sequence of ten garden rooms disperses crowding rather than pooling it at the entry.

6. Ethics of Synthetic Personas — Stated Plainly

These six neighbors are AI-synthesized composites built from public statistics and a simulated survey; no real individual was profiled, and the "~400 responses" are modeled, not collected. Synthetic personas can encode stereotype; each was therefore reviewed by the human team for dignity and plausibility, and carries a confidence label. Before design freeze, Dubai Municipality co-design workshops and a real bilingual survey will validate or retire each persona — the simulation re-runs on real data, and real voices override synthetic ones wherever they differ.
