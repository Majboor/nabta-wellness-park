A. Circulation Hierarchy — Movement for Every Body

In a wellness park, circulation is the programme: walking, wheeling and jogging are Nabta's primary health offer. One legible spine and two subordinate layers ensure every visitor — stroller, wheelchair user, jogger — can complete the loop with dignity.

  • Primary: the Living Shade Loop. 1.0 km figure-eight, 3.5 m shared path, continuously shaded. A 1.0 m jogger lane is marked in terracotta-pigmented resin on the outer edge; cross-slope ≤2% throughout, gradients ≤1:21, no ramps anywhere.
  • Secondary: garden paths. 1.8 m stabilised, firm and slip-resistant paths entering each of the ten garden rooms, with 1.8×1.8 m passing/turning bays at every junction.
  • Tertiary: desire-line shortcuts. Compacted decomposed-granite links across the loop's waist and lawn edges, formalising natural shortcuts; all step-free.

Walk times (1.0 m/s accessible pace): entry plaza → play dune 3 min; → sensory wadi 5 min; full loop 17 min walking, 6 min jogging. No point is more than 4 minutes from a loop exit gate.

C. AI-Assisted Circulation Validation (Human-Led)

The plan was stress-tested with an agent-based pedestrian simulation: ~5,000 synthetic visitors per scenario, with calibrated wheelchair (0.8 m/s), stroller, elderly, child and jogger profiles across Friday-peak, school-run and summer-evening demand. AI verified, humans decided:

  • Confirmed no crossing point where jogger and wheelchair flows conflict at >0.3 encounters/min; the terracotta jogger lane was moved to the outer edge as a result.
  • Validated that every simulated wheelchair agent completes the loop with rest seating ≤50 m apart and a restroom within 200 m from any point — counts are simulation-verified, not rules-of-thumb.
  • Predicted desire lines matched the tertiary shortcut layer; two AI-suggested shortcuts were rejected by the design team on CPTED sightline grounds, logged in the decision record.
D. Arrival Strategy
  • Drop-off: lay-by off the SE access road per RTA geometry — 3 vehicle lengths, level boarding, tactile paving, shade canopy — sized for the school-run peak.
  • Parking: ~40 car bays (incl. 4 accessible + 2 family) in a shaded grove lot; permeable paving.
  • Public transport: signed, shaded, step-free links to 2 RTA bus stops within 300 m.
  • Micromobility: 30 shaded bicycle racks at each of the three entrances (90 total), plus an e-scooter dock per entrance (SE, NW, NE), all outside pedestrian desire lines.
E. Service & Emergency Access
  • Loop segments engineered as a 4 m clear emergency route (path + reinforced shoulder), giving Civil Defence and ambulance reach to all ten rooms; removable bollards at the three gates.
  • W-edge service yard: screened compound with café back-of-house delivery, maintenance store, and irrigation plant; service vehicles never cross visitor space during opening hours.
  • Six paired waste/recycling points on the loop, serviced from the W yard before 7:00 am.
F. Indicative Commercial & Service Facilities Plan

Catchment reasoning: Al Safa 2 is a low-rise villa district — several thousand residents within a 10-minute walk, schools south, Sheikh Zayed Road offices minutes away. Demand peaks at dawn/evening wellness hours, the school run and winter weekends; fixed commercial space is sized to the reliable base load, flexible cart pads absorb peaks.

Activation & concession note: café and kiosks are tendered as one concession with revenue share tied to mandated opening hours (06:00–23:00), guaranteeing dawn/evening service through summer when the misted, shaded terrace keeps the SE plaza usable; cart pads flex to winter events without permanent build-out. A wellness clause obliges the operator to a healthy-menu baseline and free water refills, aligning commercial income with the park's health mission and keeping the commercial footprint under 2% of park area.

G. Safety by Design
  • CPTED: clear sightlines along the loop, understorey planting below 0.8 m at path edges, no dead-end paths, active café frontage at the main entrance.
  • Adaptive lighting: loop 20 lux, garden rooms 10 lux, planted gardens 5 lux; all 2700 K warm white, full-cutoff dark-sky-compliant fixtures, presence-dimmed after 23:00.
  • Privacy commitment: operational CCTV at entrances only; no facial recognition and no biometric analytics anywhere in the park.
