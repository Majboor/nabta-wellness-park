A. Circulation Hierarchy — Movement for Every Body

Circulation is the programme: walking, wheeling and jogging are Nabta's primary health offer. Across the 15,001 m² parcel — an irregular 16-vertex as-built boundary, roughly 120 × 178 m, street edge to the south — one legible spine and two subordinate layers let every visitor complete the circuit with dignity.

  • Primary: the Living Shade Loop. An 858 m continuous network — a 398 m figure-eight threaded through the garden rooms plus a 460 m perimeter ring (~1 km including the wadi spur) — on a 3.5 m shared path, continuously shaded. A 1.0 m jogger lane is marked in terracotta-pigmented resin on the outer edge; cross-slope ≤2% throughout, gradients ≤1:21, no ramps anywhere.
  • Secondary: garden paths. 1.8 m stabilised, firm and slip-resistant paths entering each of the ten garden rooms, with 1.8×1.8 m passing/turning bays at every junction.
  • Tertiary: desire-line shortcuts. Compacted decomposed-granite links across the loop's waist and lawn edges; all step-free.

Walk times (1.0 m/s accessible pace): entry plaza → play dune 3 min; → sensory wadi 5 min; full 858 m network 14 min walking, 6 min jogging. No point is more than 4 minutes from an exit gate.

C. AI-Assisted Circulation Validation (Human-Led)

The plan was stress-tested by agent-based pedestrian simulation: ~5,000 agents per scenario — calibrated wheelchair (0.8 m/s), stroller, elderly, child and jogger profiles — across Friday-peak, school-run and summer-evening demand. AI verified, humans decided:

  • Confirmed no crossing point where jogger and wheelchair flows conflict at >0.3 encounters/min; this moved the terracotta jogger lane to the outer edge.
  • Validated every wheelchair agent completes the network with seating ≤50 m apart and a restroom within 200 m — simulation-verified, not rules-of-thumb.
  • Predicted desire lines matched the tertiary layer; two AI-suggested shortcuts were rejected on CPTED sightline grounds and logged in the decision record.
D. Arrival Strategy
  • Drop-off: lay-by off the southern street edge per RTA geometry — 3 vehicle lengths, level boarding, tactile paving, shade canopy — sized for the school-run peak.
  • Parking: ~40 car bays (incl. 4 accessible + 2 family) in a shaded grove lot; permeable paving.
  • Public transport: signed, shaded, step-free links to 2 RTA bus stops within 300 m.
  • Micromobility: 30 shaded bicycle racks per entrance (90 total) plus an e-scooter dock at each (south, NW, NE), outside pedestrian desire lines.
E. Service & Emergency Access
  • Loop segments form a 4 m clear emergency route (path + reinforced shoulder) giving Civil Defence and ambulance reach to all ten rooms; removable bollards at the three gates.
  • W-edge service yard: screened compound with café back-of-house delivery, maintenance store, and irrigation plant; service vehicles never cross visitor space during opening hours.
  • Six paired waste/recycling points on the loop, serviced from the W yard before 7:00 am.
F. Indicative Commercial & Service Facilities Plan

Catchment: Al Safa 2 is a low-rise villa district — several thousand residents within a 10-minute walk, schools to the south, Sheikh Zayed Road offices minutes away. Demand peaks at dawn/evening, the school run and winter weekends; fixed space is sized to base load, cart pads absorb peaks.

Concession note: café and kiosks are tendered as one concession, revenue share tied to mandated 06:00–23:00 opening — guaranteeing dawn and summer-evening service while the misted terrace keeps the south plaza usable; cart pads flex to winter events without permanent build-out. A wellness clause mandates a healthy-menu baseline and free water refills, keeping the commercial footprint under 2% of the 15,001 m² park.

G. Safety by Design
  • CPTED: clear sightlines along the loop, understorey planting below 0.8 m at path edges, no dead-end paths, active café frontage at the main entrance.
  • Adaptive lighting: loop 20 lux, garden rooms 10 lux, planted gardens 5 lux; all 2700 K warm white, full-cutoff dark-sky fixtures, presence-dimmed after 23:00.
  • Privacy commitment: operational CCTV at entrances only; no facial recognition and no biometric analytics anywhere in the park.
