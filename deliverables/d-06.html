Deliverable 6 — Concept & Preliminary Masterplan

«نبتة» NABTA — Wellness Park: The Living Shade Loop. The plan sits on the true as-built parcel — an irregular 16-vertex polygon of 15,001 m² within a ~120 × 178 m envelope, street edge to the south, internal grid rotated ~47° from north — and is organized by one move: a continuous 3.5 m shaded path, a 398 m figure-eight braided into a 460 m perimeter ring for an 858 m continuous network (~1 km including the wadi spur). The design team selected the figure-eight through an open generative search: thousands of shade-element configurations scored in simulation (published in the runnable nabta-loop-optimizer repository) and four full concept variants scored against the site's real sun and wind (nabta-earth-obs — variant C won at 0.72); the leading options were stress-tested against six personas and an 1,800-visitor crowd simulation before this human-led choice, and community workshops are planned to validate it before design freeze. The loop is the park's spine and its climate device — under a canopy of ~450 trees for most of its length, under AI-form-found pergola segments where trees cannot mature quickly. It threads ten garden rooms arranged as a gradient: active rooms (entry plaza, majlis, café) cluster at the southern main entrance, with play and teen zones on the eastern band; calm rooms (picnic groves, quiet garden) retreat to the north and north-west. A sensory wadi — a planted bioswale — cuts the diagonal from the entry side to a still pool in the quiet garden, crossed by terracotta bridges where the loop passes over it.

The crossing at the centre of the figure-eight is the plan's social hinge: every circuit passes it, so the fitness grove, event lawn edge and wadi bridges share one naturally surveilled node. The network is sized for ~1,800 daily visits. Room areas below are net; the path network is counted as circulation, and buffer planting carries the acoustic berm along the road edge. All figures are preliminary and will be tested against the AED 35M cost plan in Deliverable Package 1.

Zoning notes
  • Active-to-calm gradient. Noise-generating rooms (1, 2, 10) sit within 60 m of the S entry, with the play dune (4) and teen court (5) on the eastern band; rooms 7 and 9 occupy the northern band, buffered by the event lawn and wadi in between. gradient SE→NW
  • 30 m road buffer. No seating or play surface within 30 m of the southern main road except the hardscaped entry plaza; a planted berm and the densest tree massing line the S and E edges to cut traffic noise by an estimated 6–8 dB(A).
  • Prayer-time adjacency. The majlis plaza (2) and café terrace (10) flank the main entry on the direct walking line to the neighbourhood mosque, supporting pre- and post-maghrib gathering; the quiet garden (9) offers a low-stimulus alternative.
  • School-run adjacency. Play dune (4) and teen zone (5) cluster at the NE secondary entrance on the school walking route, absorbing the 14:00–16:00 peak without crossing calm rooms; bicycle parking at all three entrances.
  • Canopy budget. ~450 trees, majority native (ghaf, sidr and date palm, with proven adaptive shade species completing the canopy) are placed by a shade-simulation model and checked by the landscape team: target 92% of the 858 m network shaded at 15:00 in peak summer (21 June) (simulation verifies 91.6%), with pergolas bridging early-year gaps until year-5 canopy closure; the mature canopy cuts perceived temperature by up to 4.5 °C. Smart irrigation on recycled water cuts irrigation demand 41% below a conventional park — with zero potable water — a deliberately conservative figure. AI-assisted, human-verified
  • Service without conflict. Maintenance access enters from the W edge, reaching the lawn and wadi directly and never crossing the loop at grade during operating hours.
Implementation phasing — 18 months, park never fully closed
